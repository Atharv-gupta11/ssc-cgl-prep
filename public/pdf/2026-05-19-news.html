RBI Monetary Policy: Repo Rate kept at 6.50% by MPC

The six-member Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) of the Reserve Bank of India, headed by Governor Shaktikanta Das, decided unanimously to keep the policy repo rate unchanged at 6.50%. The standing deposit facility (SDF) rate remains at 6.25% and the marginal standing facility (MSF) rate at 6.75%. The MPC retained its stance as 'withdrawal of accommodation' to ensure inflation progressively aligns with the 4% target while supporting growth.
Background: The RBI's MPC meets six times a year (bi-monthly) to decide the benchmark interest rate. The repo rate is the rate at which commercial banks borrow from the RBI. A higher repo rate makes borrowing expensive, reducing money supply and curbing inflation. India's retail inflation (CPI) target is 4% with a tolerance band of ±2% (i.e., 2%–6%), as set by the Government of India under the flexible inflation targeting framework introduced in 2016.
Key Terms:
  • Repo Rate: The rate at which RBI lends short-term funds to commercial banks against government securities. Currently 6.50%.
  • Reverse Repo Rate: The rate at which RBI borrows from commercial banks. It is the floor of the LAF corridor.
  • MPC (Monetary Policy Committee): A 6-member statutory body (3 RBI + 3 Government nominees) established under Section 45ZB of the RBI Act, 1934. Decisions are taken by majority vote; the RBI Governor has a casting vote.
  • CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio): Percentage of a bank's total deposits that must be kept with RBI as liquid cash. Currently 4.5%. Earns no interest.
  • SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio): Percentage of net demand and time liabilities that banks must maintain in gold, cash, or government securities. Currently 18%.
  • Standing Deposit Facility (SDF): A facility for banks to park excess funds with RBI without collateral. Introduced in April 2022. Acts as the floor of the LAF corridor.
Why It Matters for SSC: RBI policy rates (Repo, Reverse Repo, CRR, SLR, MSF, Bank Rate) are among the most frequently asked topics in SSC CGL General Awareness. Questions often ask: 'Who is the current RBI Governor?', 'What is the current repo rate?', 'How many members are in the MPC?', and 'Which section of the RBI Act established MPC?'.
Exam point: Memorise: Repo = 6.50%, SDF = 6.25%, MSF = 6.75%, CRR = 4.5%, SLR = 18%. MPC has 6 members under RBI Act Section 45ZB.

Cabinet approves PM-KISAN 18th instalment — ₹20,000 crore released

The Union Cabinet approved the release of the 18th instalment of the Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN) scheme, transferring ₹2,000 each to approximately 9.5 crore farmer families across India. The total disbursement under this instalment is approximately ₹20,000 crore, directly credited to beneficiaries' bank accounts via Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT).
Background: PM-KISAN was launched on 24 February 2019. Under this scheme, eligible farmer families receive ₹6,000 per year in three equal instalments of ₹2,000 each. The scheme is 100% funded by the Central Government and is implemented by the Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers' Welfare. It uses the Aadhaar-seeded bank account system for DBT. As of 2025, over ₹3 lakh crore has been disbursed to more than 11 crore registered farmers since inception.
Key Terms:
  • PM-KISAN: Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi — a Central Sector scheme providing ₹6,000/year in 3 instalments to small and marginal farmer families. Launched: 24 Feb 2019.
  • Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT): A Government of India initiative to transfer subsidies and benefits directly to the bank accounts of beneficiaries. Launched on 1 January 2013 to reduce leakages.
  • Central Sector Scheme: A scheme 100% funded by the Central Government (unlike Centrally Sponsored Schemes which have cost-sharing with states).
  • Aadhaar Seeding: The process of linking Aadhaar number with a bank account to enable identity verification for DBT.
Why It Matters for SSC: PM-KISAN is one of the most asked government schemes in SSC CGL. Questions test: launch year, amount per instalment, implementing ministry, and the DBT mechanism. Also asked: difference between Central Sector and Centrally Sponsored Schemes.
Exam point: PM-KISAN: ₹6,000/year in 3 instalments of ₹2,000. Central Sector scheme. Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers' Welfare. Launched 24 Feb 2019.

ISRO successfully launches PSLV-C58 carrying XPoSat satellite

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) successfully launched the PSLV-C58 mission from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC-SHAR) in Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh. The primary payload was XPoSat (X-ray Polarimeter Satellite), India's first dedicated polarimetry mission to study cosmic X-ray sources. XPoSat was placed in a 650 km Low Earth Orbit (LEO). The PSLV-C58 also carried 10 payloads on the POEM-3 (PSLV Orbital Experimental Module) platform.
Background: PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle) is ISRO's workhorse rocket, operational since 1993. It has completed 60+ missions with a remarkable success rate. XPoSat is only the world's second X-ray polarimetry satellite after NASA's IXPE (launched 2021). ISRO was established on 15 August 1969 and is headquartered in Bengaluru. The current Chairman of ISRO is S. Somanath. India became the 4th country to soft-land on the Moon with Chandrayaan-3 in August 2023.
Key Terms:
  • PSLV: Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle — ISRO's reliable 4-stage launch vehicle. Can carry up to 1,750 kg to Sun-Synchronous Polar Orbit (600 km).
  • GSLV: Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle — heavier rocket with cryogenic upper stage for communication satellites in Geosynchronous Transfer Orbit.
  • XPoSat: X-ray Polarimeter Satellite — India's first mission to study polarisation of X-rays from celestial sources like black holes, neutron stars, and pulsars.
  • SDSC-SHAR: Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh — India's primary spaceport, located on Sriharikota island between Pulicat Lake and Bay of Bengal.
  • POEM: PSLV Orbital Experimental Module — converts the spent 4th stage of PSLV into a stabilised platform for in-orbit experiments.
Why It Matters for SSC: ISRO missions are a staple in SSC CGL Science & Tech. Questions ask: launch vehicle used, satellite name and purpose, launch site, ISRO chairman, and India's space firsts (Chandrayaan-3, Aditya-L1, Gaganyaan).
Exam point: ISRO HQ: Bengaluru. Chairman: S. Somanath. Launch site: SDSC-SHAR, Sriharikota, AP. PSLV = polar orbits, GSLV = geosynchronous orbits.

Government launches Unified Pension Scheme (UPS) for central employees

The Union Cabinet approved the Unified Pension Scheme (UPS) for central government employees, effective from 1 April 2025. Under UPS, employees retiring after 25 years of service will receive an assured pension of 50% of the average basic pay of the last 12 months. For those with 10–25 years of service, the pension will be proportionate. A minimum pension of ₹10,000/month is guaranteed for employees with at least 10 years of service. The Government contribution increases to 18.5% (from 14% under NPS).
Background: The Old Pension Scheme (OPS) was replaced by the National Pension System (NPS) for employees joining after 1 January 2004. NPS is a defined-contribution scheme (market-linked returns, no guaranteed pension) regulated by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA). UPS is a hybrid model that restores assured pension benefits while retaining some NPS features.
Key Terms:
  • UPS (Unified Pension Scheme): Assured pension scheme for central govt employees. 50% of avg basic pay (last 12 months) after 25 years. Effective 1 April 2025.
  • NPS (National Pension System): Defined-contribution pension scheme launched 1 Jan 2004 for new govt employees. Market-linked returns, regulated by PFRDA.
  • OPS (Old Pension Scheme): Defined-benefit pension: 50% of last drawn basic pay as pension. Discontinued for new joiners post-2004.
  • PFRDA: Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority — statutory body established under PFRDA Act, 2013 to regulate NPS and pension sector.
Why It Matters for SSC: Pension-related questions frequently appear in SSC CGL Polity & Economy. Expect questions on: NPS vs OPS, PFRDA's role, UPS eligibility criteria, and government contribution percentages.
Exam point: UPS: 50% of avg basic pay after 25 yrs, min ₹10,000/month, govt contributes 18.5%. NPS regulated by PFRDA (est. 2013). OPS ended for post-2004 joiners.

Finance Ministry: GST collections cross ₹1.87 lakh crore in April 2025

Gross GST revenue collections for April 2025 stood at ₹1,87,346 crore, marking a 12.4% year-on-year growth. CGST accounted for ₹33,100 crore, SGST for ₹41,200 crore, IGST for ₹98,200 crore, and Cess for ₹14,846 crore. The number of GST returns filed hit a record high, indicating improved compliance.
Background: GST (Goods & Services Tax) replaced multiple indirect taxes and was implemented on 1 July 2017 under the 101st Constitutional Amendment Act, 2016. It is governed by the GST Council (Article 279A) chaired by the Union Finance Minister. GST has a 4-tier rate structure: 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%. Essential items are either exempt or taxed at 0%. Gold and precious stones attract 3%.
Key Terms:
  • GST: Goods & Services Tax — a unified indirect tax subsuming Central Excise, Service Tax, VAT, and others. Implemented 1 July 2017.
  • CGST: Central GST — collected by the Central Government on intra-state supply of goods/services.
  • SGST: State GST — collected by the State Government on intra-state supply.
  • IGST: Integrated GST — collected by Centre on inter-state supply and imports. Later apportioned between Centre and states.
  • GST Council: Constitutional body under Article 279A. Chaired by Union Finance Minister with state finance ministers as members. Recommends GST rates.
  • 101st Amendment Act: Constitutional amendment that introduced GST (Articles 246A, 269A, 279A). Passed in 2016, effective 2017.
Why It Matters for SSC: GST is one of the most asked Economy topics in SSC CGL. Memorise: implementation date, constitutional article, council composition, rate slabs, and the difference between CGST/SGST/IGST.
Exam point: GST: 1 July 2017, 101st Amendment, Article 279A (Council). Rate slabs: 5%, 12%, 18%, 28%. Council chaired by Union Finance Minister.
